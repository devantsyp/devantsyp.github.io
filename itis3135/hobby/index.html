Home

Breakdancing, also known as b-boying, is a style of street dance combining elements from acrobatics, urban dancing, and even martial arts. From its inception in the early 1980s in the Bronx in African-American and Puerto Rican communities, breakdancing has undergone remarkable changes.

What started as a raw and expressive street dance quickly evolved into a global phenomenon - breakdancing has moved from underground battles to international competitions, with events like the Red Bull BC One and the upcoming inclusion in the 2024 Paris Olympics solidifying its place as a respected sport and art form.

How to Start

To start breakdancing, you'll need determination. Here are some tips to get you started:

  • Start with the basics. Begin by learning to "toprock," which is the standing portion of breakdancing. Then, focus on getting smooth and precise footwork down, mastering basic moves like 6-step.
  • Develop core strength and flexibility. Breakdancing requires strong muscles, especially core, arms, and legs, as well as great flexibility. Begin a workout routine that incorporates olympic-style lifts that focus on explosive power, train your endurance, and improve flexibility.
  • Work on your timing and rhythm. Breakdancing is all about rythm. Practice dancing to music to get used to the beats by matching your moves to the music's rythm.
  • Maintain consistency. Consistent practice is the only way to improve. Practice on a weekly basis, seek help from others, and don't be afraid to fail.
